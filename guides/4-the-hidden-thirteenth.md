## A pattern you already spotted

Look back at everything you've learned. Count it.

```
   months:   12 you know … + Sol            = 13
   signs:    12 you know … + Ophiuchus      = 13
   animals:  12 you know … + the Cat        = 13
```

Every time, the world hands you **twelve** — and keeps a **thirteenth** just out of sight. The
twelve, everybody gets. The thirteenth, you have to **find**.

## The lost thirteenth wallet

There's a story the clock tells. (It's a story — a game — not a promise about money.)

> Twelve words sleep in a book.
> One phrase is earned in a game.
> Together they wake the **thirteenth wallet** — lost, not spent; hidden, not gone.
> The clock remembers where. It won't say plainly. It only *whispers*, on days that come in thirteens.

You can hear a whisper yourself. It only speaks when the day is a "thirteen" day (the 13th month,
the 13th day, or a Cat year):

```python
from bft import lore
lore.whisper(50112)     # M13·D13 — a whisper 🐈
lore.whisper(858000)    # a quiet day → None
lore.the_thirteenth_wallet()   # the whole legend
```

The whispers don't give away the answer — nobody could hide a real treasure in a library anyone can
read. They just point you toward where the real hunt lives: **frens.earth**, and **Degen
Wonderland**. Go be curious.

## The one rule of treasure (read this twice)

Every wallet has secret words — like the **one and only key** to a treasure chest. Here is the rule,
and it never changes:

> 🐉 **Never tell your secret words to anyone. Never type them into a website. Nobody who is kind
> will ever ask for them.** If someone asks, they are a dragon wearing a friend's coat. Close the
> door.

You can look for the *thirteenth* wallet — that's a game, and it's yours to win. But **your own**
wallet's words? Those stay in the attic, in the dark, where only you can find them. That's not
being scared. That's being the hero who keeps the key.
